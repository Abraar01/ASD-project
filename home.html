Understanding Autism Spectrum Disorder

Watch this informative video to learn more about ASD and its impact on children and families

How ASD Affects Children

Social Communication Challenges

Children with ASD often struggle with social interactions, maintaining eye contact, understanding social cues, and developing age-appropriate friendships.

Repetitive Behaviors

Many children with ASD engage in repetitive movements or behaviors, have intense interests in specific topics, or insist on strict routines.

Sensory Sensitivities

ASD often involves unusual reactions to sensory input - either hypersensitivity or hyposensitivity to sounds, lights, textures, or smells.

Why ASD Occurs

Genetic Factors

Research shows that ASD has a strong genetic component, with certain gene mutations increasing risk. However, no single gene causes ASD.

Environmental Influences

Certain environmental factors during pregnancy may increase ASD risk, including advanced parental age, prenatal exposure to certain medications, or complications during birth.

Brain Development

Differences in brain structure and function, including how neurons connect and communicate, play a role in ASD development.

Treatment and Early Diagnosis

Behavioral Therapies

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) and other behavioral interventions can help children develop social and communication skills while reducing problematic behaviors.

Speech and Language Therapy

Many children with ASD benefit from specialized therapy to improve communication skills, both verbal and nonverbal.

Importance of Early Diagnosis

Early detection leads to early intervention, which can significantly improve outcomes. Children who receive support earlier often show greater progress in developing communication and social skills.
